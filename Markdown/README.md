## 🎯 Project Vision

> [!NOTE]
> **Personal Hub**: This is my digital space where I showcase my work, share my knowledge through blog posts, and provide information about who I am.
> It's designed to be a beautiful, modern experience that reflects my approach to web development.

### 🧠 The Knowledge Seeker's Approach

I believe in the power of **contextual learning** and **relationship building** between information.
My approach to development and life revolves around:

-   🔍 **Active Learning**: Constantly absorbing information from diverse sources
-   🧩 **Context Building**: Creating connections between seemingly unrelated concepts
-   🎯 **Probability-Based Sorting**: Evaluating options based on probability
-   💡 **Knowledge Preservation**: Never dismissing any information, no matter how seemingly useless or unlikely, every piece of knowledge can become valuable

> [!TIP]
> **Why This Matters**: In a world of rapid technological change, the ability to connect dots across domains is more valuable than deep expertise in any single area.

## 💭 My Beliefs & Values

### 🏢 Corporate Culture vs Personal Motivation

I believe in **user-centric design** and **personal motivation** over corporate-driven development.
Too often, projects are built to satisfy CEOs and shareholders rather than actual users.
I focus on creating technology that serves people first, not profit margins.

### ⚖️ Work-Life Balance & Passion

**Passion should never be killed by corporate demands.**
I believe in maintaining work-life balance while staying motivated through personal drive, not external pressure.
Quality work comes from genuine interest and sustainable practices, not burnout culture.

### 🎯 User-Centric Development

Every decision in my development process prioritizes the human experience.
I build for users, not for corporate checkboxes or arbitrary metrics.
Technology should enhance lives, not complicate them.

### 🤝 Trusting Experts & Building Relationships

**People in charge should trust their experts and build relationships with them.**
Too often, non-experts demand what they think is important without understanding the technical realities.
I believe in collaborative environments where expertise is respected, not overridden by uninformed decisions.

### Frontend

-   **Next.js** - React framework with App Router

## 🚧 Development Status

> [!WARNING]
> **Currently in Development**: This project is actively being rebuilt from Astro to Next.js for better React integration and reduced development friction.

### Why the Switch?

After starting with Astro, I realized that for a React-focused developer, working with Next.js and Vercel, Astro was creating more friction than value.
While Astro has excellent use cases, this project benefits more from the React ecosystem's consistency and tooling.
