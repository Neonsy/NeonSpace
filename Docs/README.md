This is my corner of the web, a personal space to collect work, write openly, and connect ideas across design and engineering

My general goal is to create freely, share in public, and keep a living record of what I am learning

> [!NOTE]
> With the amount of information I consume, it will never be a complete record, but let's not talk about that

## The NeonSpace Philosophy

> "In the vast digital cosmos, every piece of knowledge is a star waiting to be connected. The most unlikely information can become the bridge to breakthrough insights"

### Personal by Design

NeonSpace is personal by design

It is not meant to be a clean, corporate-style portfolio

I do not believe in separating the work from the person completely

That kind of presentation feels like it comes from an older world, where the work was allowed to matter but the person behind it was expected to stay invisible

My projects are shaped by my interests, values, hobbies, frustrations, curiosities, and the connections I make between them

This site will include professional work, personal thoughts, experiments, and whatever else feels relevant to the way I learn and create

I want NeonSpace to feel honest

Not overly polished, not artificially neutral, and not afraid to let the person shine through

### The Knowledge Seeker's Approach

- **Active Learning:** I am constantly taking in ideas from different places, people, and experiences, filtered by my understanding
- **Context Building:** I like finding connections between things that do not seem tied together at first, because some synergy requires discovery and experimentation
- **Evaluation / Iteration:** I tend to explore possibilities by testing, comparing, remixing, and letting ideas evolve over time
- **Knowledge Preservation:** I do not dismiss information, because even small or unlikely details can become useful later

### Beliefs, Values, and Practice

#### User-Centric Development

I care about building things around real human experience

Good technology should make life clearer, easier, richer, or more interesting, not just satisfying a checklist

#### Corporate Culture vs Personal Motivation

I am more motivated by usefulness, curiosity, and care than by corporate performance theater

Too many projects are shaped around executives, metrics, or shareholders before they are shaped around the people who actually use them

I want my work to serve people

#### Trusting Experts and Building Relationships

I believe good work comes from trust, communication, and respect for expertise

People make better decisions when they listen to those who understand the details, and teams work better when relationships matter as much as outcomes

#### Work-Life Balance and Passion

I care about sustainable passion

Work should not drain the life out of the people doing it

The best projects usually come from genuine interest, enough space to think, and the freedom to follow what keeps you engaged
